All About Dolphins

Fun Facts

> Intelligent Communicators: Dolphins are known to have complex, personalized communication systems,
with scientists observing exchanges that resemble human conversations. They are one of the few species capable
of recognizing themselves in a mirror, indicating high self-awareness.

> Unique Sleeping Habits: Dolphins sleep with only one half of their brain at a time, allowing them to remain
conscious enough to breathe while resting. This also means one eye remains open to watch for predators or obstacles.

> Built-in Speedsters: Dolphins are fast swimmers, with some species reaching speeds of over 25 miles per hour.
Their sleek skin regenerates roughly every two hours, which reduces drag and helps them move efficiently through the water.

Types of Dolphin Environments

> Coastal and Shallow Waters: Many dolphin species, such as the common bottlenose, prefer shallow coastal areas, bays, and
estuaries that offer abundant food sources. These habitats often provide shelter and protection, though they also bring
dolphins into closer contact with human activities.

> Open Ocean (Pelagic Zone): Several species inhabit the deep, open oceans, sometimes hundreds of miles from land, and are
known to travel vast distances to follow food sources. These environments are characterized by deep water, where dolphins often
form large pods and may dive to significant depths to hunt.

> Freshwater River Systems: Several species, such as the Amazon river dolphin (boto), live exclusively in freshwater rivers and
lakes, with some adapting to navigate flooded forests. These dolphins often have poor eyesight, relying instead on specialized echolocation
to hunt in murky, silt-heavy waters.
